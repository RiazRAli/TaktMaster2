TaktMaster Rules & Teaching Guide

Use this simulator to learn takt time, flow, WIP control, bottlenecks, and labor flexibility.
Game objective: produce at the customer demand rate without overproducing, building excess WIP, wasting labor, or running out of money. The best result is not maximum output; it is stable, demand-paced flow.

How to Win

  • Maintain actual output pace close to the customer takt requirement for 60 seconds.
  • Stay cash-positive while doing it.
  • Keep producing recently; the game needs enough completed-unit data before it will count a stable win.
  • Floaters may move around. A station looking temporarily unbalanced will not block a win if actual output is truly meeting takt.

Important: Do Not Overproduce

Producing much faster than takt is overproduction, one of the Lean wastes. It may look good at first, but it can hurt your final score through WIP, lead time, overproduction time, and idle-resource penalties.

Demand, Takt, and Output Pace

Demand Rate is how many units the customer wants per minute.

Takt Requirement is the allowed time per unit:

Takt = Available Time ÷ Customer Demand

Example: 60 seconds ÷ 6 units = 10 seconds/unit.

Actual Output Pace is what your line is really achieving based on completed units.

Station Terms

  • CT: station cycle time before worker capacity and improvements.
  • Effective Pace: station seconds/unit after workers, flex availability, and upgrades.
  • Bottleneck: the one current station that most limits flow. The red warning appears only when a station is slower than takt.
  • Starved: station has capacity but no work waiting.
  • Blocked: station finished work but cannot send it forward because the downstream transit buffer is full.

Workers and Flex Availability

  • Hire adds a fixed worker at that station, up to the max worker limit.
  • Lay Off removes a fixed worker.
  • Flex % applies only to the last fixed worker at that station. Earlier workers stay full-time.
  • Example: 3 workers at Flex 50% = 2 full workers + 1 half-time worker = 2.5 effective workers.
  • A half-time flex worker is not penalized if that available half-time is fully used.

Manage Workers

  • Use Manage Workers to move a fixed worker between stations without laying off and rehiring.
  • Reallocation has a nominal cross-training/reassignment cost.
  • You can train a Floater for selected stations or all stations. This costs more but gives flexibility.
  • Floaters have a home station. They return home when not needed elsewhere and support the highest-need trained station when their home is idle.

Transit WIP Buffers

  • The boxes between stations control how much WIP is allowed in transit/downstream waiting areas.
  • Use + to allow more WIP. This may prevent starvation but can increase lead time.
  • Use − to tighten flow. This may reduce WIP but can block upstream stations.
  • If current WIP reaches the buffer limit, the upstream station becomes blocked until space opens.

Upgrades

  • Add Capacity Slot: raises the station worker limit; you still need to hire or move a worker.
  • Kaizen: reduces cycle time and is usually best for the current bottleneck.
  • Standard Work: reduces variation at a station.
  • 5S / Layout: reduces transport time between stations.

Difficulty and Random Events

  • Easy: lower costs and less variation.
  • Medium: normal costs and variation.
  • Max: random events can occur, such as machine slowdowns, transport delays, or material shortages.
  • The difficulty slider locks after the round starts.

Final Score

  • Takt Alignment: how well actual output matched takt.
  • Profit: cash discipline after labor, hiring, upgrades, penalties, and revenue.
  • WIP Control and Lead Time: how well you avoided excess inventory and long waits.
  • Utilization Discipline: penalizes paid available capacity sitting idle too long.
  • Overproduction Control: penalizes running much faster than customer demand.
